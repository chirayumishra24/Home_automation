DEFINITION

What is automation?

Automation is the process of making things operate automatically using circuits and programs, without constant human intervention. It transforms manual tasks into efficient, self-regulating systems.

OVERVIEW

Automation means using technology to control everyday devices automatically. Instead of switching things on and off by hand, automation uses sensors, controllers, and simple computer programs to run these tasks independently. This helps save energy, reduce costs, and make life easier.

EVERYDAY EXAMPLES

Smart Classrooms

An automated system with a motion sensor can turn off lights and fans when no movement is detected, preventing energy waste.

Garden Irrigation

Moisture sensors detect dry soil and activate water pumps, conserving water by watering only when needed.

Did you know?

Automation can save up to 30% on electricity bills in schools by preventing lights and fans from being left on accidentally.

CASE STUDY

Faro Barcelona

Ceiling fans in schools using automated control systems to optimize airflow and energy consumption based on occupancy.

NATURAL AUTOMATION

Human Body

Functions like heartbeat, breathing, and digestion happen automatically using nerve signals and hormones.

Human heart beats ~100k times daily.

Nature

Sunrise, sunset, the water cycle, and animal migration operate independently based on natural signals.

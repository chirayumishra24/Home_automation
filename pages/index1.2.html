History & Evolution

Overview

The history of automation shows a transformation from manual tasks into automated systems, evolving over centuries from simple devices to complex AI-integrated machines.

Ancient Beginnings

Ancient devices like sundials and water clocks used natural elements (sun position or water flow) for timekeeping and routine management. These early mechanisms introduced the concept of using physical processes to measure and trigger actions.

Mechanical Era

Mechanical clocks, the Jacquard Loom, and steam power drove early automation during the Industrial Revolution. The Jacquard Loom's punch cards later inspired early computer programming concepts.

Electrification & Computing

Electronic controls and computing enabled programmable machines, industrial controllers, and networked systems that manage factories and services.

AI, Robotics & Smart Cities

AI and robotics enable smart homes, smart cities, and systems that manage transport, health, and energy. Example: Pixies robot.

Future Trends

Predictive automation driven by AI and machine learning will anticipate needs and optimize processes, creating new jobs while displacing some manual roles and raising ethical considerations.
